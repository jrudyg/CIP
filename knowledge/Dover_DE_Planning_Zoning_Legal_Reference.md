## 5. Subdivision Requirements

### Three-Stage Process

#### Stage 1: Conceptual Plan (Current Stage)
- **Purpose:** Initial layout and feasibility
- **Requirements:**
  - Basic lot configuration
  - Street layout
  - Utility concepts
  - Stormwater management areas
  - Identification of waivers needed

#### Stage 2: Preliminary Plan
- **Purpose:** Detailed engineering
- **Requirements:**
  - Detailed grading plans
  - Utility designs
  - Stormwater calculations
  - Traffic studies (if required)
  - Environmental assessments

#### Stage 3: Final Plan
- **Purpose:** Construction documents
- **Requirements:**
  - Construction specifications
  - Bonding/surety
  - Legal documents
  - Final plat for recording

### Street Design Standards

#### General Requirements
- **Right-of-way width:** Minimum 60 feet (residential)
- **Pavement width:** 
  - Standard: 38 feet
  - With parking on both sides: 38 feet
  - With parking on one side: 30 feet
  - No parking: 26 feet minimum

#### Cul-de-sac Specifications
- **Maximum length:** Varies by jurisdiction (typically 400-1,000 feet)
- **Turnaround diameter:** 
  - Right-of-way: 130 feet minimum
  - Pavement: 100 feet minimum
- **Fire access requirements:** Per State Fire Marshal

### Infrastructure Requirements

#### Utilities
- Water and sewer connections required
- Underground utilities preferred
- Coordination with utility providers

#### Stormwater Management
- Compliance with DNREC regulations
- On-site management required
- Maintenance agreements necessary

#### Sidewalks
- Generally required on both sides
- Waivers possible with justification
- ADA compliance mandatory

## 6. Waiver Procedures

### Legal Basis for Waivers
- **Authority:** Article VII, Section A of Land Subdivision Regulations
- **Standard:** Practical difficulties or unnecessary hardship
- **Requirement:** Not contrary to public interest

### Types of Waivers

#### Commonly Requested
1. Street width reductions
2. Cul-de-sac length extensions
3. Sidewalk eliminations (partial or complete)
4. Lot frontage modifications
5. Right-of-way width reductions

#### Your Specific Waiver
**Percentage of Lots on Cul-de-sac**
- Standard limitation on number/percentage of lots
- Safety and circulation concerns
- Emergency access considerations

### Waiver Process

#### Step 1: Application
- Identify waiver need in conceptual plan
- Provide written justification
- Document unique circumstances

#### Step 2: Staff Review
- Planning staff analysis
- DAC comments
- Staff report preparation

#### Step 3: Planning Commission
- Public hearing
- Commission discussion
- Recommendation to Council

#### Step 4: City Council
- Final decision
- Conditions may be imposed
- Findings required

### Justification Criteria

#### Evaluate Against:
1. **Unique physical constraints**
   - Topography
   - Existing development
   - Environmental features
   - Utility locations

2. **Public benefit**
   - Better design outcome
   - Environmental protection
   - Cost-effectiveness
   - Safety improvements

3. **Consistency with plans**
   - Comprehensive Plan goals
   - Neighborhood character
   - Development patterns
