## RED FLAGS BY CONTRACT TYPE

### **MUTUAL NDAs (Session-Enhanced)**
**DEALBREAKERS:**
- Non-compete clauses (should never be in NDA)
- Perpetual term (max 5-7 years typical)
- Assignment to competitors permitted
- No return/destruction of information clause

**HIGH RISK:**
- One-way confidentiality obligations
- Overly broad definition of confidential information  
- No exceptions for publicly available information
- Residuals clause favoring one party
- Attorney fees provision (favors deeper pockets)

**MEDIUM RISK (Session-Added):**
- **Asymmetric assignment rights** (one party can assign to affiliates, other cannot)
- **Attorney fees favoring either party** (creates disproportionate cost risk for vendors)
- **Jury waiver clauses** (can be strategic positioning opportunity)
- **Unlimited confidentiality survival** (3-5 year limits generally acceptable)

**LOW RISK (Session-Validated):**
- **Grammar/formatting errors** (easy wins, 99% success rate)
- Standard mutual confidentiality provisions
- Reasonable survival periods with defined limits

**STRATEGIC CONSIDERATIONS FOR VENDORS (Session-Added):**
- Complete deletion often more strategic than cosmetic formatting changes
- Selective pushback demonstrates standards without damaging relationship
- Focus on competitive protection and reasonable business terms
- Balance relationship preservation with commercial protection

**Dependencies:** Definition of Confidential Information → Exceptions → Return/Destruction → Term

### MSAs (Master Service Agreements)
**DEALBREAKERS:**
- Unlimited liability with no cap
- Exclusive commitment without minimum guarantees
- Auto-renewal without notice period
- Vendor displacement rights (can take your customers)
- Unilateral termination for convenience
- IP assignment of pre-existing IP

**HIGH RISK:**
- Payment terms before delivery/acceptance
- No right to terminate for cause
- Indemnification without knowledge qualifiers
- Most Favored Nation without reciprocity
- Change orders without approval rights
- No dispute escalation before legal action

**Dependencies:** 
- Payment Terms ↔ Acceptance Criteria ↔ Warranty
- Liability Cap ↔ Indemnification ↔ Insurance
- Term ↔ Termination ↔ Transition Assistance
- IP Ownership ↔ License Back ↔ Improvements

### PROJECT/SOW AGREEMENTS
**DEALBREAKERS:**
- Scope without clear deliverables
- Acceptance without objective criteria
- No change order process
- Payment without milestones
- IP transfers before final payment

**HIGH RISK:**
- Time and materials without cap
- Delay penalties without force majeure
- Warranty without time limits
- Travel costs without approval
- Resources "subject to availability"

**Dependencies:**
- Deliverables ↔ Acceptance ↔ Payment ↔ IP Transfer
- Timeline ↔ Resources ↔ Change Orders
- If standalone: Incorporate all MSA concerns

### RESELLER AGREEMENTS  
**DEALBREAKERS:**
- Direct customer relationship for vendor
- No customer protection period
- Territory can be modified unilaterally
- Quotas without corresponding exclusivity
- Vendor can appoint competing resellers anytime

**HIGH RISK:**
- Price changes without notice
- No commission on renewals
- Marketing fund contributions mandatory
- Business plan submission requirements
- Customer data shared with vendor
- Non-compete disguised as exclusivity

**Dependencies:**
- Territory ↔ Exclusivity ↔ Quotas ↔ Competing Resellers
- Customer Protection ↔ Commissions ↔ Direct Sales Rights
- Pricing ↔ Margins ↔ Payment Terms

### SaaS AGREEMENTS
**DEALBREAKERS:**
- No data portability/export rights
- Data deletion immediately on termination
- Unlimited price increases
- No SLA or service credits
- Unilateral feature removal rights

**HIGH RISK:**
- Auto-renewal without notice
- Usage overage charges uncapped
- No security breach notification
- Data location not specified
- No backup/disaster recovery commits
- Integration breaking changes without notice

**Dependencies:**
- SLA ↔ Service Credits ↔ Termination Rights
- Data Rights ↔ Portability ↔ Termination ↔ Transition
- Pricing ↔ Renewal ↔ Usage Limits

## SCHEDULE IMPACT TRIGGERS

**These patterns cause delays:**
- "Subject to approval" (no timeframe)
- "Commercially reasonable efforts" (undefined)  
- "From time to time" (vague frequency)
- "Periodically" (no schedule)
- "As may be required" (open-ended)
- "In its discretion" (no standards)
- "Reasonably promptly" (no deadline)
- "Good faith" (without specifics)

**Fix with:**
- Specific business day deadlines
- Deemed approval if no response
- Defined approval criteria
- Regular meeting schedules
- Specific notice periods

## WEASEL WORDS DICTIONARY

**Undefined Obligations:**
- "Best efforts" → "commercially reasonable efforts"
- "Assist with" → "provide [specific support]"
- "Coordinate" → "manage and deliver"
- "Support" → "[specific actions]"

**Escape Hatches:**
- "To the extent" → "shall"
- "May include" → "shall include"
- "Generally" → [delete]
- "Substantially" → "materially" or specific %

**Hidden Expansions:**
- "Including without limitation" → "including"
- "Such as but not limited to" → "specifically"
- "Inter alia" → "including"
- "For example" in definitions → [delete examples]

## QUICK DECISION RULES

**Accept if all true:**
- Risk is manageable
- Business value exceeds risk
- Alternative protections exist
- Not a DEALBREAKER item

**Negotiate if any true:**
- Unbalanced risk/reward
- Standard terms available
- Leverage supports change
- Creates operational burden

**Strategic Negotiate (Session-Added):**
- Positioning opportunity exists
- Demonstrates thoughtful engagement
- Builds respect without relationship harm
- Shows standards while preserving partnership

**Walk away if any true:**
- DEALBREAKER with no movement
- Liability exceeds deal value  
- Compliance impossible
- Better alternatives exist
