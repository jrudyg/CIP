BROKER REPRESENTATION AGREEMENT

This Broker Representation Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of April 1, 2025 (the "Effective Date") by and between:

PREMIER MANUFACTURING CO., a Michigan corporation ("Principal")

and

INDUSTRIAL SALES PARTNERS LLC, an Ohio limited liability company ("Broker")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Principal manufactures industrial equipment and machinery; and

WHEREAS, Broker has established relationships with potential customers in the industrial sector; and

WHEREAS, Principal desires to engage Broker to identify and develop sales opportunities;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. APPOINTMENT

1.1 Engagement. Principal hereby engages Broker as its authorized sales representative to identify, solicit, and develop customers for Principal's Products.

1.2 EXCLUSIVE TERRITORY. Principal hereby grants Broker the EXCLUSIVE right to represent Principal's Products in the states of Ohio, Indiana, Kentucky, and West Virginia (the "Territory"). During the term of this Agreement, Principal shall NOT appoint any other broker, representative, or sales agent to represent the Products in the Territory. Principal shall NOT sell directly to any customer located in the Territory without Broker's involvement and commission participation.

1.3 EXCLUSIVITY PERIOD. The exclusive arrangement shall remain in effect for the full term of this Agreement and any renewal periods. Principal acknowledges that Broker is investing significant resources based on this exclusivity commitment.

1.4 Protected Accounts. In addition to territorial exclusivity, Broker shall have exclusive rights to the accounts listed in Exhibit A ("Protected Accounts") regardless of where such accounts are located. Principal shall not solicit or accept orders from Protected Accounts without Broker's involvement.

2. BROKER'S DUTIES

2.1 Sales Activities. Broker shall:
    (a) Actively promote and solicit orders for the Products;
    (b) Maintain regular contact with existing and prospective customers;
    (c) Provide technical support and product demonstrations;
    (d) Attend industry trade shows and events;
    (e) Maintain a professional sales organization.

2.2 Reporting. Broker shall provide:
    (a) Monthly activity reports detailing customer contacts and opportunities;
    (b) Quarterly pipeline forecasts;
    (c) Prompt notification of competitive activities.

2.3 Non-Competition. During the term and for 24 months thereafter, Broker shall not represent any products that directly compete with Principal's Products in the Territory.

3. PRINCIPAL'S DUTIES

3.1 Support. Principal shall:
    (a) Provide product training and technical support;
    (b) Supply marketing materials and samples;
    (c) Process orders promptly and accurately;
    (d) Maintain product quality and availability.

3.2 Pricing. Principal shall provide Broker with current pricing and shall give 30 days notice of any price changes.

3.3 Lead Referral. Principal shall refer all leads and inquiries from the Territory to Broker.

3.4 EXCLUSIVITY PROTECTION. Principal agrees to actively protect Broker's exclusive territory rights. If Principal becomes aware of any attempts to circumvent Broker's exclusivity, Principal shall notify Broker immediately and take appropriate corrective action.

4. COMPENSATION

4.1 Commission Rate. Broker shall earn a commission of 8% of net sales on all Products sold to customers in the Territory or to Protected Accounts.

4.2 House Accounts. The accounts listed in Exhibit B are "House Accounts" on which Broker shall earn a reduced commission of 3%.

4.3 Commission on Exclusivity Violations. If Principal sells directly to any customer in the Territory in violation of Broker's exclusivity, Broker shall be entitled to the full commission as if Broker had made the sale, plus liquidated damages equal to the commission amount.

4.4 Payment Terms. Commissions are payable monthly, within 15 days after Principal receives payment from the customer.

4.5 Commission on Termination. Upon termination, Broker shall continue to receive commissions on:
    (a) Orders placed before termination;
    (b) Orders from customers developed by Broker, for 12 months post-termination;
    (c) Reorders from Broker's customers for 6 months post-termination.

5. TERM AND TERMINATION

5.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for three (3) years.

5.2 Renewal. This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one-year terms unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least 180 days prior to expiration.

5.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other party:
    (a) Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 60 days;
    (b) Becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy;
    (c) Engages in fraud or illegal conduct.

5.4 Termination by Principal. Principal may terminate for convenience upon 180 days written notice, provided that Principal shall pay Broker a termination fee equal to 12 months of average commissions.

5.5 Effects of Termination. Upon termination:
    (a) Principal shall pay all earned commissions within 30 days;
    (b) Broker shall return all Principal materials and confidential information;
    (c) Post-termination commission rights under Section 4.5 shall survive.

6. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

6.1 Principal Warranties. Principal represents and warrants that:
    (a) It has full authority to enter into this Agreement;
    (b) The Products conform to published specifications;
    (c) No other broker has exclusive rights in the Territory.

6.2 Broker Warranties. Broker represents and warrants that:
    (a) It has full authority to enter into this Agreement;
    (b) It has the capability to effectively represent the Products;
    (c) It is not subject to any conflicting obligations.

7. INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Principal Indemnification. Principal shall indemnify Broker against claims arising from:
    (a) Product defects or failures;
    (b) Principal's breach of this Agreement;
    (c) Infringement of third-party intellectual property rights.

7.2 Broker Indemnification. Broker shall indemnify Principal against claims arising from:
    (a) Broker's negligence or willful misconduct;
    (b) Broker's breach of this Agreement;
    (c) Broker's unauthorized representations.

8. CONFIDENTIALITY

8.1 Confidential Information. Each party shall maintain the confidentiality of the other party's proprietary information.

8.2 Customer Information. All customer information developed by Broker shall be jointly owned by the parties. Upon termination, each party shall retain the right to use such information.

9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 Mediation. Any dispute shall first be submitted to mediation.

9.2 Arbitration. If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in Cleveland, Ohio.

9.3 Injunctive Relief. Either party may seek injunctive relief to protect its rights under this Agreement without first pursuing mediation or arbitration.

10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 Independent Contractor. Broker is an independent contractor, not an employee or agent of Principal.

10.2 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of Michigan.

10.3 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

10.4 Amendment. This Agreement may only be amended in writing signed by both parties.

10.5 Assignment. Neither party may assign this Agreement without prior written consent.

10.6 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses below.
